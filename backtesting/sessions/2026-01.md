### Concept of the Month: Concentration Caps

A concentration cap limits how much of your portfolio any single holding can represent — in our case, 25%. The idea is simple: if one investment tanks, it can't take more than a quarter of your money with it. But caps create a side effect: when a position grows through gains (not because you bought more), it can block you from adding to it even if you still believe in it. That's exactly what happened with IWDA this month — it's at 23.3%, which looks like there's room, but buying even one more share at €113 would push it to ~28%, breaching the cap. The cap forces you to diversify whether you like it or not. Over time, as your total portfolio grows, the cap relaxes naturally: 25% of €5,000 is €1,250, giving much more room than 25% of €2,400. This is one reason small portfolios feel more constrained than large ones — the rules bite harder when the numbers are smaller.
