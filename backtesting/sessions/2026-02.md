### Concept of the Month: Bull-Market Backtests

A backtest that covers only a rising market tells you how a strategy performs when things go right — but not how it handles things going wrong. Over the past 12 months, this portfolio never experienced a significant drawdown: no position hit a stop-loss, no emergency exit was needed, and cash was never deployed defensively. That is partly good design (gold decorrelation, defensive positions, cash buffer) and partly luck (no recession, no flash crash, no sustained bear market). The real test of a portfolio isn't how much it makes when markets rise — it's how much it keeps when markets fall. The post-mortem should evaluate: what would have happened in a March 2020-style shock? A 2022-style rate spike? These are the scenarios where the 30% cash floor would prove its worth — or where the structural under-deployment might have been a hidden blessing.
