### Analysis

The tariff shock is real and durable even with the pause. The 10% baseline on all US imports is now the floor, and the 145% China tariff approaches embargo conditions. The US faces an uncomfortable policy bind: the Fed cannot cut into a supply-side inflation shock without risking credibility, nor hike into a demand shock without accelerating recession.

Europe is structurally better positioned for reasons unrelated to tariffs. The German debt brake reform is the most significant European fiscal event in a decade — a multi-year pipeline of infrastructure and defence spending operating through domestic demand channels largely insulated from US tariff policy. The ECB easing cycle adds accommodation on top.

**Tailwinds**: German fiscal stimulus, ECB easing, EUR strength (real purchasing power), gold performing as hedge, European domestic demand insulated from tariffs.

**Headwinds**: EUR/USD at 1.13 compresses exporter margins, China exposure risk (luxury, autos), VIX at 30 means risk appetite hasn't genuinely recovered, US recession risk elevated.
